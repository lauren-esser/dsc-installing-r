Imagine your you and your colleagues are teaching a lesson with the tips.csv data set to group of students from different countries (England, Japan, India) who are about to take their first visit to the United State of America. To get your students used to the idea of how much they are expected to tip their servers, we need to make new columns that convert the USD column to pounds, yen, and Australian dollars.

Note were going to do this the tidyverse way!

converted_tips <- tips %>% 
  select(-X1) %>% 
  mutate(gbp_total= total_bill * 0.81) %>%
  mutate(gbp_tips = tip * 0.81) %>%
  mutate(yen_total = total_bill * 106) %>%
  mutate(yen_tips = tip * 106) %>%
  mutate(aus_total = total_bill * 0.64) %>%
  mutate(aus_tips = tip * 0.64)

Lets also make a ggplot and recreate whats in the seaborn documentation for the fun of it with a few extra lines of code.

tips %>%
  mutate(time = factor(time, levels = c("Lunch","Dinner"))) %>%
  ggplot(aes(
    x = total_bill,
    y = tip,
    color = smoker,
    shape = smoker,
    size = size
  )) +
  scale_shape_manual(values=c(16, 4)) +
  geom_point(aes(shape = smoker)) +
  facet_wrap( ~ time) +
  labs(x = "Total Bill",
       y = "Tip",
       title = "Total Bill vs Tip",
       subtitle = "Split by Time and Smoker") +
  scale_color_manual(values=c("#4C71B0", "#DF8C5F"))
